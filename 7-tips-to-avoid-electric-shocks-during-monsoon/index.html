Why Is Monsoon Season Risky for Electrical Systems?

During the rainy season, moisture seeps into soil, walls and even electrical fittings. Water is a natural conductor of electricity, so any exposed wire or damaged insulation becomes a silent hazard. Here's what typically goes wrong during monsoons:

  • Waterlogging around pumps and panels
  • Cracked or low-quality wire insulation
  • Uncovered junction boxes and exposed splices
  • Improper earthing or no grounding system
  • Use of substandard cables that degrade over time

These small oversights can lead to serious electric shocks, pump failures and even life-threatening accidents.
