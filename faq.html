  • How do I store cables before installation?

    Cables should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. Make sure they are not exposed to harsh chemicals or moisture, as this can damage the insulation and reduce their lifespan.

  • What factors affect the lifespan of a cable?

    The lifespan of a cable can be influenced by factors like the type of insulation, the environmental conditions, the amount of current being carried, and how well the cable is installed. Proper handling, installation, and maintenance can significantly extend the lifespan of cables.

  • What is the difference between copper and aluminium conductors in cables?

    Copper conductors have better conductivity and lower resistance than aluminium, which makes them more efficient for power transmission. However, aluminium is lighter and more cost-effective, making it ideal for large-scale transmission lines. Gimpex offers both options based on your needs.

  • How can I identify the correct cable size for my project?

    Choosing the right cable size depends on factors such as the voltage and current requirements of your system, the length of the cable run, and the type of equipment being used. Our technical team can help guide you in selecting the appropriate size based on your specific needs.

  • How do I maintain and care for my cables?

    Proper maintenance of cables involves regular inspections for signs of wear, ensuring they are securely installed, and avoiding unnecessary bending or twisting. It's important to keep cables clean and free from debris, and to replace any damaged or worn cables promptly.
