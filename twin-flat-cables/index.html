Twin Flat Cables for Reliable and Neat Electrical Installations

Engineered for Seamless Electrical Distribution

Twin Flat Cables are the backbone of countless residential and commercial electrical installations. Designed to carry power efficiently and safely across short to medium distances, they provide an ideal solution for fixed wiring where compactness, flexibility and reliability are critical. Whether concealed behind walls or mounted on surface panels, Twin Flat Cables offer a clean and practical solution for lighting, appliance connections and general-purpose power distribution.

Gimpex Twin Flat Cables are crafted with a strong emphasis on material purity, dimensional accuracy and electrical integrity. Each cable features two parallel copper conductors, individually insulated with high-grade PVC, enclosed in a flat, durable sheath that simplifies installation and enhances safety. Their symmetrical structure makes them easier to lay out during installation and ideal for routing through narrow spaces without compromising performance.

Backed by over two decades of expertise in cable manufacturing, Gimpex Twin Flat Cables are trusted by electricians, contractors, OEMs and infrastructure developers who demand consistent performance in day-to-day wiring tasks.

Why Twin Flat Cables Are Preferred for Fixed Wiring Applications?

When it comes to electrical distribution in modern buildings, clarity, space optimization and safety are essential. Twin Flat Cables offer a structured approach to wiring, allowing two insulated copper wires to run parallel in a single configuration, maintaining uniform spacing and minimizing cross-talk or electromagnetic interference.

Electricians appreciate the time saved during installation, as the flat design eliminates the need for repeated cable alignment and ensures a tidy layout within conduits, ducts or surface-mounted trays. They are especially effective in residential wiring, switchboards, junction boxes, lighting connections and domestic power circuits where neatness and efficiency matter.

Core Features of Gimpex Twin Flat Cables

When wiring demands simplicity without compromising safety, Twin Flat Cables deliver performance that professionals can rely on. Here’s what sets Gimpex apart:

  • Optimal Current Carrying Capacity: With a precision-engineered copper core, the cable delivers high conductivity and thermal efficiency. This allows for stable power flow, supporting lighting circuits and low-load electrical appliances efficiently.

  • Flame Retardant and Heat Resistant: Built to meet stringent safety benchmarks, Gimpex Twin Flat Cables exhibit self-extinguishing properties and can withstand elevated temperatures without deformation or performance loss. This adds a critical layer of safety in domestic and commercial installations where fire protection is non-negotiable.

  • Minimal Signal Interference: Twin Flat Cables are designed to maintain consistent spacing between conductors, reducing electromagnetic interference. This ensures steady power delivery, especially in environments where multiple cables run close to each other.

  • Availability in Multiple Core Configurations and Sizes: To cater to a wide variety of applications, Twin Flat Cables are available in different conductor sizes ranging from 0.5 sq.mm to 4 sq.mm, in both solid and stranded variants. This gives electricians and project engineers the flexibility to choose the right specification for their specific load and installation requirements.

  • Smooth Installation Experience: The flat parallel layout of the conductors allows for space-saving and tangle-free routing through conduits, walls and trunking systems. This structure is particularly useful in concealed wiring, where space and aesthetics matter most.

Ideal Applications of Twin Flat Cables

Gimpex Twin Flat Cables are engineered to suit a wide range of low voltage wiring tasks, including:

  • Lighting Circuits: Used extensively in home and office lighting, offering stable illumination and secure connections.

  • Power Extensions: Supports small appliances, fans and low-power devices with reliable current distribution.

  • Switchboard Wiring: Used for connecting switches, sockets and distribution boards in domestic and small commercial setups.

  • Surface and Concealed Installations: Performs equally well in both exposed runs and inside conduits, thanks to the rugged sheath and flexible build.

  • Temporary Setups and Event Wiring: Can be used in short-term setups where quick deployment and easy removal are required without sacrificing safety.
