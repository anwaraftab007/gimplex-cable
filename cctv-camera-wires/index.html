CCTV Wires (Camera Cable) for Seamless Surveillance

Clarity. Continuity. Confidence in Every Connection.

In the world of modern surveillance, the clarity of the footage depends just as much on the cable as it does on the camera. A high-definition security camera is only as effective as the signal it transmits and a stable, interference-free connection is non-negotiable when safety is on the line. Gimpex CCTV Wires are built to ensure flawless signal transmission, consistent power delivery and uninterrupted surveillance across all types of installations, from residential properties to large-scale commercial infrastructures.

With a focus on quality, durability and signal purity, the CCTV cable range supports analog and digital camera setups, offering a dependable foundation for any security network. Whether used in multi-storey buildings, retail stores, housing societies, warehouses or industrial zones, the wires are crafted to meet the highest performance standards required by installers, system integrators and security professionals.

Why Quality CCTV Cable Makes All the Difference?

The effectiveness of a CCTV system depends on its weakest link. Poor signal quality, power drops or electromagnetic interference can compromise critical footage, often when it matters most. That’s why investing in reliable camera wiring is essential for maintaining continuous, high-quality video transmission without distortion or interruption.

Gimpex CCTV Wires are designed to deliver unmatched signal integrity over long distances, resist environmental stresses and maintain consistent performance across varying voltage loads. Each spool undergoes rigorous testing and is manufactured with precision to ensure every meter of cable contributes to a seamless surveillance experience.

Key Advantages of Gimpex CCTV Wires

CCTV wiring is not just about connectivity; it plays a critical role in the overall reliability and performance of the entire surveillance ecosystem. With that in mind, Gimpex offers a product built on three pillars: signal clarity, installation efficiency and environmental resilience.

  • High-Purity Copper Conductors: Manufactured using electrolytic grade copper, the conductors inside the cable offer low resistance and high conductivity. This translates to efficient power transfer and minimal signal loss, allowing cameras to function effectively even over extended cable runs.

  • Double Shielding for Signal Integrity: Signal interference can lead to blurred or distorted visuals, especially in electrically noisy environments. The CCTV wires come with a robust dual-shielding configuration consisting of aluminium foil and a braided mesh. This provides an effective barrier against EMI and RFI, ensuring crisp, clear footage even in high-density electrical installations.

  • Integrated Video and Power in One Cable: Designed for easy installations, each cable combines a coaxial core for video transmission with twin power wires, allowing both video signal and DC power to run through a single unified line. This eliminates the need for multiple cables, simplifying layout and reducing clutter, especially in ceiling or wall-mounted systems.

  • UV and Weather Resistant Outer Jacket: Outdoor installations demand a higher level of protection. The outer jacket is built using UV-stabilized, weather-resistant PVC that withstands heat, moisture and exposure to sunlight. It ensures long service life and uninterrupted protection against environmental wear.

  • Consistent Output in Varied Conditions: Performance remains steady across a wide range of voltage fluctuations and weather extremes. Whether cameras are installed indoors under AC ducts or outdoors exposed to wind and rain, the wire maintains consistent signal transmission and power flow.

Engineered for Professional Installations

The wire design aligns with the expectations of CCTV professionals and system integrators. Every roll offers tangle-free winding, standard colour coding and uniform construction, helping technicians save time and reduce installation errors. Whether you're setting up analog CCTV, HD cameras or hybrid systems, the wire provides backward compatibility with older technology and future-ready performance for newer systems like AHD, HDCVI and HDTVI.
